Crypto Events: Why You Shouldn't Attend

There are a million reasons why you should attend crypto events, but you only need one reason to skip them. Here are five.

Going to a crypto expo takes more courage than you think, and while these events have a range of benefits — advancing your career, showcasing your skills, networking with the industry's most influential — sometimes you just want to stay home, and we can't stop you.

5 Reasons You Shouldn't Attend Crypto Events

Yes, we're an events company. Yes, we sell tickets to some of the most fabulous Web3 events ever. But just because you don't want to attend doesn't mean you should feel bad about it.

Here are five reasons to stay in bed:

1. You Embrace FOMO

Are you feeling the buzz? Excited to meet industry icons and network with other professionals? Chances are, if you're not attending, you'll be feeling a little FOMO — and we don't blame you.

From meeting hundreds of Web3 builders, founders, and other professionals, to learning how to accelerate your growth within the blockchain arena, there's a lot to miss out on.

We're here to tell you it's okay. Our advice: stick to your guns. Commit to not attending. You can't fear missing out if you never intended to show up.

2. Maybe Parties Aren't Your Thing

Hey, we get it. Not everyone's a party animal, and the idea of attending yet another crowded event might not be your cup of tea. From crypto newbies to the industry's most influential, everyone's going to be there — and if that stresses you out, there's nothing wrong with staying in.

We'll post updates on social media so you can stay informed from the comfort of your home. Of course, you won't get to meet all the fantastic people in attendance. Still, there's only a small chance you'll miss out on a life-changing opportunity. NBD.

3. Crypto Is Boring

We know. Sometimes, crypto can be a total snooze-fest. From technical jargon to complex algorithms, blockchain really can make your head spin. Why deal with all this complex decentralised infrastructure when real-time global payments already exist?

If it ain't broke, don't fix it. Our financial system works just fine. It's all good, and even if it does come crashing down, you know how to handle it… right?

4. You're A Lone Wolf

The blockchain industry is built heavily on collaboration, with decentralised, distributed participants coming together to make magic. But there are also lone warriors discovering innovative utilities for blockchain. If you're one of them, the benefits of attending upcoming crypto events are significantly muted.

Adventures feel more special when you keep them to yourself, and since you aren't the type to ask for help, why expend effort building powerful connections? After all, the industry's biggest names made it on their own, didn't they?

5. Your Comfort Zone Is Big Enough

Stepping out of your comfort zone is the key to personal growth, but let's be honest — your comfort zone is already pretty impressive. Attending an event full of intimidating professionals takes courage, but your comfort zone has cozy blankets, your favourite snacks, and Netflix. Why disrupt the equilibrium?

Sure, our events make those passionate about Web3 feel right at home, but it's not the same as being right at home. The coffee might be spectacular, and the food may be gourmet — but nothing beats microwaved leftovers and a homemade cuppa.

Be Unstoppable

We can't stop you from staying at home, but we can outline the awesome parts of crypto events in a fun way. Jokes aside, we know everyone isn't the biggest fan of blockchain events, but the benefits of attending one are undeniable.

Deep down, you possess an unstoppable force driving you toward learning more about crypto. You've taken the first step into the world of digital currencies, and that's a bigger step than you give yourself credit for. It's why you're reading this article in the first place.

With every transaction, every trade, the blockchain industry is rewriting the rules of the game. Crypto events are about celebrating the brave warriors on the front lines. Whether you're an introvert, not a 'people person,' or just don't like leaving the house — we hope to see you at one of our upcoming events, for everyone's sake.
