Unblock 2021's Winning Blockchain Hackathon DApps

A look at the standout decentralised applications built at Unblock 2021 — Octaloop's blockchain hackathon in partnership with Nano and NEAR.

Applications are the fuel of any software-based ecosystem's growth. The blockchain industry is bursting at the seams with revolutionary ideas and dedicated people. While the space has certainly matured from its humble origins over a decade ago, bottlenecks around development resources have stifled its acceleration toward mainstream use.

Bitcoin's market capitalisation is worth more than nearly every world bank, but transactions can still take hours to confirm. Scalability has been an issue for cryptocurrencies since the advent of blockchain technology, but as more developers look at the problem, the solution appears to be almost within reach.

With Unblock 2021, we had two core goals: incentivise experienced blockchain developers to create innovative applications, and inspire newcomers to have a look at what the fuss is all about. Partnering with Nano and the NEAR protocol, Unblock 2021 was a hotbed of wild ideas.

Dapper Applications

Team Fayco's Nano Point of Sale (now NaoPay) project was a very popular submission — a clear winner under the Nano Blockchain for Payments theme with its feeless instant payment solution for merchants, clean interface, and great UX design. Customers pay by scanning a QR code, eliminating the hassle of tracking long account addresses while offering all the advantages of a secure, decentralised network.

The winning submission for the Payments theme's KaiOS track was the elaborately named "Simplifying Nano payments on KaiOS with mobile number based virtual addresses." Team GG designed the application to map a user's Kaiak wallet address to their phone number, creating an added level of convenience for making payments on the Nano network.

While it didn't win any prizes, Ms. Lily's Volunteers was another standout submission in the Blockchain for Social Initiatives theme — using the NEAR blockchain to connect NGOs and other charitable organisations with volunteers on the network.

Among the other submissions was an application that allows users to create websites and instantly begin accepting Nano payments. While the project's scope is sizeable, one developer managed to pull it off using two plugins (CoinEmbed and Catnip) to implement its core functions.

Another solo project was VajraNet, developed to create a secure and scalable stablecoin streaming network for essential commodities. Using a fork of the Rainbow Bridge protocol from NEAR, VajraNet offers a privacy-focused algorithmic stablecoin platform that accounts for both supply and demand-side dynamics.

Events to Eventualities

Unblock 2021 was both productive and educational, familiarising more novice blockchain developers with the field and encouraging more experienced participants to get creative with their ideas. Hackathon events are perfect for spreading awareness in blockchain developer communities, and by sparking interest in more developers around the world, blockchain is slowly creating more robust solutions for the modern financial system.

A hackathon doesn't just challenge your knowledge of blockchain or your programming skills — it also challenges your mental fitness to develop fresh ideas and come up with effective solutions for days on end.

We've always taken a more organic approach to making decentralised networks a significant part of everyone's lives. Having hosted India's largest blockchain meetups, our dedicated communities are built from real human interactions, and we're constantly on the lookout for new projects and ideas to showcase at our events.
